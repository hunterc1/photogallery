Tunnels on Campus

They might look scary during certain times of day and night; however nothing is more friendly to a Hokie on a rainy day than a walk in a tunnel. Stay dry Hokies!

The Path Less Traveled

As Hokies, we are always trying to make new ways to walk around campus. The concrete path seem to long? Just take the dirt path closest to you, they are located all over campus!

Hokie Stone

Virginia Tech is known for its "Hokie Stone." The majority of buildings around campus are built and decorated with this stone. Such beautiful and amazing architecture!

Inside of Torg

Ever wondered what goes on inside that ever popular hall called "Torgersen"? Now you know, students can be found studying in here!

Bus Stop

Want to know the best part about being a Hokie? It is the amazing BT transit buses that haul students, and community members all over town! Here you can see the bus loading up, waiting to pull out.

Torg Bridge

One of the most popular sights on campus, the Torg bridge! Inside the bridge you can find students suffering in quiet, in effort to study for their exams or finish a project.

Fall in Blacksburg

One of the many perks to being a Hokie is getting to experience the different seasons in Blacksburg. The trees turn such pretty colors in the fall, and make campus look like a Crayola box!

Residential Life

Do not be afraid! Living on campus, or even visiting, can be scary. VT has such a big campus that it can be easy to get lost and confused. Thanks to helpful signs like this one, Hokies can easily navigate their way across campus.

Burruss Hall

Probably the most known place on campus. Burruss Hall is home to the biggest auditorium on campus. In front of the hall a monument is set up for those who lost their lives in the 2007 shooting.

Walking Across Campus

Walking around campus can sound boring. With the amazing scenery and architecture that Virgina Tech has to offer, Hokies can always have something interesting to look at while they stroll across campus!

Everyday Events

As Hokies walk across campus, they run into all sorts of different events and activities. Daily, you can find different groups out and about on campus with some sort of event going on. Just look for the nearest booth, walk up, and ask them what they are doing!

Leaves are Falling

Blacksburg is at its finest in the fall of the year. Hokies will find the ground covered in an assortment of beautiful leaves that can make a stroll to class enjoyable, and maybe even boost their spirits!
